# Privacy Policy for Anti-Paste Bypass

**Effective Date:** July 21, 2026

Thank you for choosing to use **Anti-Paste Bypass** ("the Extension"). We take your privacy very seriously. This Privacy Policy outlines what information the Extension collects and how it is used.

## 1. Data Collection and Usage
Anti-Paste Bypass is designed to function entirely locally on your device. 
- **No Analytics or Tracking:** We do not use Google Analytics, tracking pixels, or any telemetry software to monitor your usage.
- **No Data Transmission:** The Extension does not send any of your text, passwords, typing history, or browsing data to any external servers.
- **Local Storage Only:** The text you paste into the Extension and your typing speed preferences are saved locally on your machine using the Chrome `storage.local` API. This is strictly to provide the core functionality of the Extension (such as the recent history feature). 

## 2. Permissions Justification
To function properly, the Extension requests the following permissions:
- **`activeTab` & `scripting`:** Used strictly to inject the typing simulation script into the specific webpage you are currently viewing when you explicitly trigger the extension via the context menu. We do not read or monitor the content of the webpages you visit.
- **`contextMenus`:** Used to add the "Paste with Anti-Paste Bypass" option to your right-click menu.
- **`storage`:** Used to save your pasted text and speed settings locally on your hard drive.

## 3. Third-Party Services
We do not share, sell, or rent any data to third parties, simply because we do not collect any data.

## 4. Changes to This Privacy Policy
We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page. By continuing to use the Extension, you agree to the updated policy.

## 5. Contact Us
If you have any questions or suggestions about our Privacy Policy, do not hesitate to contact the developer on the Chrome Web Store page.
